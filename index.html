Ramadan Kareem

Sahri and ifter time schedule

Ramadan is a special month for Muslims worldwide. It's a time for fasting from sunrise to sunset, promoting self-discipline and empathy. Families come together for joyous iftar meals every evening, sharing food and strengthening connections. More than just not eating, Ramadan encourages acts of kindness and charity, fostering personal growth. Our website celebrates the beauty of Ramadan, its significance, and the sense of community it brings.

  • Dua for Roza (Fasting)

    This is the dua for opening fast (or dua for keeping fast), which is what we called Sahur (Sahoor). The dua in Arabic is as follows:

    وبصوم غد نویت من شهر رمضان

    Transliteration :Wa bisawmi ghadinn nawaiytu min shahri Ramadan. [Source: Abu Dawud]

    translate :I intent to keep fast today for the month of Ramadaan

  • Dua for Iftar (Iftar Dua)

    This is the dua for opening fast (or dua for keeping fast), which is what we called Sahur (Sahoor). The dua in Arabic is as follows:

    واللهم إني لك صمت وبك أمنت وعلى رزقك أفطرت

    Transliteration :Allahumma inni laka sumtu wa bika aamantu [wa ‘alayka tawakkaltu] wa ‘ala rizq-ika aftarthu [Source: Abu Dawud]

    translate :O Allah. I fasted for You and believe in You and I break my fast with Your sustenance
